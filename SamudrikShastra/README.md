# Samudrik Shastra — सामुद्रिक शास्त्र (Palmistry / Body Reading)

**Status: In progress — texts being sourced**

Samudrik Shastra is the science of reading physical characteristics of the body — hand lines (Hasta Samudrika), facial features (Mukh Samudrik), and general body marks (Ang Lakshana) — to understand a person's nature and destiny.

The tradition integrates three sub-disciplines:
- **Hasta Samudrika** — palmistry (hand lines, mounts, finger shapes)
- **Mukh Samudrika** — physiognomy (facial features)
- **Ang Lakshana** — body marks (moles, birthmarks, general form)

The name *Samudra* (ocean) refers to the legendary sage Samudra, the traditional authority of this shastra.

## Notes

Approximately 600 Sanskrit manuscripts on Samudrik Shastra exist across Indian manuscript repositories. The majority are undigitized and held in private collections or state manuscript libraries (BORI, Oriental Research Institute Mysore, etc.).

The Brihat Samhita (Varahamihira, 6th CE) — under `Samhita/` — contains detailed chapters on bodily marks and their interpretation, predating the specialized Samudrika texts.
